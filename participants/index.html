Willams de Lima Costa

Universidade Federal de Pernambuco

Bio:
Willams de Lima (Will) is a PhD candidate at the Centro de Informática in Universidade Federal de Pernambuco, Brazil, and an R&D Project Leader at Voxar Labs. His research is focused on human behavior recognition and description under the supervision of Estefanía Talavera (University of Twente, The Netherlands) and Veronica Teichrieb (Universidade Federal de Pernambuco, Brazil). Will also collaborate with research partners in The Netherlands and Canada, as well as with industry partners such as HP, Samsung, and Volkswagen. At Voxar Labs, he leads a research team dedicated to developing approaches for emotion recognition in unconstrained scenarios, applying these technologies to solve real-world problems. Will is also an active member of the scientific community, acting as a reviewer and committee member for several national and international scientific conferences and journals such as IEEE Access and Journal on Interactive Systems. His dedication also extends to the mentoring of students in the fields of AI in LatinX communities.

Research Interests:
Human behavior recognition

Sebastián André Paucar Mendoza

Universidad Nacional de Ingeniería

Bio:
My name is Sebastian Paucar, and I am a fourth-year student of Physics Engineering at the National University of Engineering in Peru. Currently, I am undertaking an academic exchange at the National Autonomous University of Mexico, and soon I will be embarking on a research internship at the University of Alberta, focusing on the application of machine learning in drug discovery. I am passionate about everything related to deep learning, particularly its applications in biomedical engineering, bioinformatics, and quantum computing applied to solid-state physics, that is, quantum hardware. Currently, I serve as a Quantum Intern at QuantumQuipu, where I work on building quantum circuits to simulate the thermal response of a spin chain governed by a 2D Heisenberg Hamiltonian. I employ techniques such as block encoding and amplitude amplification to study the formation of skyrmions.

Research Interests:
Computer Science, Artificial Intelligence, Computer Vision, Quantum Computing, Quantum Information, Quantum Machine Learning
